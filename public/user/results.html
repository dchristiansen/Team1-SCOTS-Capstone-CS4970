Graph Information

These graphs display the asynchrony and interval length of each tap in your session.

Asynchrony is how far away your tap was from the actual correct beat. It is normal for this value to drift over time when the beat is silent.

Interval length is the rate you tap at with your internal imagined rhythm. This should stay near the center line with some variation.

If your asynchrony or interval length is within 10% of the ideal value, the point will fall between the green lines. This is the ideal area to be.

If the value is within 15% of the ideal value, it will fall between the yellow lines.

If the value is within 20% of the ideal value, it will fall between the red lines.

Anything with a variation beyond 20% will fall outside the red lines.

The vertical pink and green lines separate phases with and without sound.

The first phase has sound on. A pink vertical line marks a transition where the sound turns off. A green vertical line marks a transition where the sound turns back on.

A score is displayed at the top of the page. This score is calculated based on the interval lengths. Staying in the green area will maximize your score.
